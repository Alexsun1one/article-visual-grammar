# Aesthetic Quality Bar

Use this file to make the generated image extremely good-looking without losing accuracy.

Beauty is not decoration here. Beauty is what makes the reader want to look, understand, and remember.

## The Quality Formula

```text
beautiful article diagram =
accurate idea
+ obvious reader path
+ disciplined visual tokens
+ concrete image quality harness
+ generous whitespace
+ one focal surprise
+ typography-ready label zones
+ no unnecessary complexity
```

## Beauty Score

Rate every planned or generated image from 0 to 5.

### 5: Publish-Grade

- one idea reads in three seconds
- the form is the right form, not merely an available form
- visual tokens are semantically consistent
- the focal area is unmistakable
- whitespace feels intentional
- labels have clean homes
- there is one memorable twist
- nothing important is fake, vague, or decorative
- the prompt translated taste into visible art direction: material, grid, palette, line behavior, typography, negative space, and anti-default-AI constraints

### 4: Good

- clear and attractive
- minor label, spacing, or color issue
- no structural confusion

### 3: Usable But Flat

- accurate but not memorable
- looks a little like a template or course slide
- needs stronger focal action, better spacing, or a clearer token system

### 2: Confusing

- too many ideas
- weak reader path
- chart or spatial form does not clarify the point
- labels float or compete

### 1: Decorative

- pretty but not useful
- metaphor overwhelms accuracy
- form choice feels arbitrary

### 0: Reject

- wrong meaning
- fake data
- copied style
- mascot-first
- unreadable

Only 4 or 5 should be delivered as final. A 3 may be kept as draft with repair notes.

## Five Beauty Levers

### 1. Focal Hierarchy

Use one of:

- largest object
- strongest carrier
- most isolated module
- one accent color
- one guide-object light
- one cutaway reveal

Do not use all at once.

### 2. Whitespace As Meaning

Whitespace can mean:

- pause before conclusion
- separation between states
- distance between roles
- uncertainty around a path
- breathing room around the focal node

Avoid filling every corner. A quiet corner often makes the image feel premium.

### 3. Material Coherence

Choose one material family:

- paper layers
- soft vector slabs
- field atlas ink
- clear-line picture-book objects
- transparent glassy lens
- miniature terrain
- tabletop model
- technical plate

Do not mix glossy 3D, sketch lines, paper cutouts, and UI cards in one image.

### 4. Typography Readiness

Even if final text will be overlaid later, the image must reserve:

- one L1 focal label zone
- 2-5 L2 object label zones
- 1-3 L3 state marks
- enough internal padding
- no busy texture under text

For Chinese: label containers should be slightly wider than the visible draft label needs.

### 5. One Focal Surprise

Add one small, truthful clever move:

- the carrier becomes a bridge, thread, river, or rail
- a layer is peeled back to reveal the hidden cause
- evidence dots become beads on the path
- the risk wedge physically pinches a flow
- the map route detours around a pressure field
- a Prism Lantern illuminates only the decisive node
- a scorecard row becomes a shelf holding proof slips
- a room floor path shows attention moving between zones
- the result leaves residue, not just a checkmark

The surprise must clarify the idea. If it only adds charm, remove it.

## Accuracy Before Charm

Do not make a visual clever by changing the article's claim.

Bad cleverness:

- turning uncertainty into certainty
- making a side point look like the thesis
- using risk color for ordinary emphasis
- inventing data or proportions
- making a metaphor imply causality when the article only says correlation
- using characters to create emotion when the text does not support it

Good cleverness:

- makes the relation easier to see
- uses the article's own nouns and verbs
- turns an abstract relation into a physical one
- leaves enough ambiguity when the article is uncertain

## High-End Composition Rules

- Use a 6-column or 8-column invisible grid.
- Keep outer margins generous.
- Align label containers to object edges, paths, layer tabs, or floor zones.
- Use one large focal module and smaller supporting modules.
- Let curves and carriers be graceful, not mechanical.
- Keep secondary scaffolding faint.
- Avoid centered symmetry unless the form is pyramid, matrix, scorecard, or comparison.
- Use asymmetry for editorial freshness.
- Avoid full-bleed clutter for article body images; these should breathe inside prose.

## Color Taste Rules

Default palette:

- Paper: warm white or pale neutral
- Ink: charcoal, not pure black
- Quiet: mist gray or pale blue-gray
- Passage: clear blue or cyan-blue
- Evidence: amber or muted yellow
- Risk: coral or warm red
- Growth: mint or calm green
- Human: violet, rose, or muted plum
- Focus: one of the accent roles, not a new rainbow color

Avoid:

- neon rainbow palettes
- beige-only diagrams
- red/green-only state systems
- all-blue enterprise diagrams
- purple gradient dominance
- saturated color behind small text

## Spatial Beauty Rules

Spatial forms should feel like editorial models, not game screenshots.

Use:

- isometric or gentle three-quarter view
- soft shadows for separation
- labels on layer edges and surfaces
- shallow depth of scene
- one readable camera angle
- muted material texture

Avoid:

- cinematic lighting
- dramatic perspective
- realistic office stock imagery
- tiny labels on receding surfaces
- decorative 3D that distorts quantities

## Simplicity Can Be Premium

Prefer simple when:

- the article has one strong sentence
- the point is emotional
- the mechanism has only two states
- exact text matters more than spatial complexity
- the image will sit inside a dense article

Simple premium forms:

- one object plus state stamp
- two-state split
- three-step carrier
- one small scorecard
- one cutaway reveal
- one route with one obstacle

## Repair Moves

If accurate but boring:

- add one focal surprise
- turn arrows into a carrier object
- make one local mechanism visible
- add a small residue or consequence

If beautiful but unclear:

- remove 30-50% of objects
- choose a simpler rung
- strengthen one carrier
- attach labels to surfaces

If too complex:

- split into sequence + evidence
- turn network into 3 clusters
- turn framework into 4-6 modules
- move supporting ideas to a second image

If too stiff:

- round the modules
- vary module size
- use one organic carrier
- add material texture lightly
- add one guide-object reveal

If too cute:

- remove characters
- reduce expressive props
- strengthen grid and labels
- make charm come from the relation, not decoration

## Final Gate

Before delivery, answer:

```text
beauty gate:
- score:
- what makes it accurate:
- what makes it understandable:
- what makes it visually desirable:
- what was removed to keep it simple:
- one focal surprise:
- remaining risk:
```
