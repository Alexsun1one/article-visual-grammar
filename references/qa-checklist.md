# QA Checklist

## Shot List QA

- Does each shot have one reader takeaway?
- Is the article structure summarized before shot decisions?
- If the user supplied a full article, was it segmented and scored before the shot list?
- Are skipped sections explicitly named with reasons?
- Does the package have a rhythm and roles, instead of one diagram per section?
- Is there a paragraph-to-shot mapping, including sections intentionally not illustrated?
- Does each shot state its package role: thesis anchor, mechanism, tension/failure, evidence/comparison, human consequence, or summary?
- Is the visual structure named?
- Is the form selection named: simplest sufficient rung, why not simpler, why not more complex?
- Is there a compact or full figure spec before generation?
- Is there an explicit accuracy risk?
- Is the complexity budget small enough for one image?
- Is the diagram form named from the catalog, including family and specific form?
- Is the diagram coverage family named: sequence, transformation, hierarchy, choice, territory, system, evidence, quantity, overlap, human situation, work/life system, hidden mechanism, constraint, accumulation, or taxonomy?
- Does the form choice include spatial mode: flat / isometric / cutaway / room / terrain / tabletop / orbit?
- Does the form choice explain why alternatives were rejected?
- Does the form choice include a beauty move that avoids stiff PPT layout?
- Is there a visual token plan: color roles, line roles, line patterns, shape roles, label containers, emphasis rule?
- Do colors and line styles have semantic meaning instead of decorative use?
- Is the visual harness explicit: output family, form family, spatial mode, style lineage, abstraction, material, line behavior, color system, composition engine, human presence, and typography mode?
- Is the image quality harness explicit: material family, grid/margins, focal hierarchy, palette discipline, typography/overlay plan, negative space contract, and anti-default-AI constraints?
- Is exactly one composition stability preset selected, with fixed layout, max anchors, max labels, focal module, quiet zones, and forbidden drift?
- Does the selected output family fit the article job: article body figure, diagram/infographic, editorial/social, or technical/product?
- Does the style lineage improve comprehension, hierarchy, memory, mood accuracy, or package unity?
- Is the style lineage stated as transferable principles rather than "in the style of X"?
- Is there an anti-copy boundary that forbids living-artist imitation, copyrighted contemporary illustrator imitation, named artwork layouts, signature compositions, and signature marks?
- Is the diagram form still primary, with style acting as a controlled layer on top?
- Is there a beauty gate with target score, focal surprise, and what was removed to keep it simple?
- Is the beauty score target 4+ for generated deliverables, not merely "acceptable"?
- Is the metaphor world chosen because of the article, not habit?
- Are the core objects concrete?
- Is there a connection grammar?
- Is there a line / relation plan?
- Is the chart or diagram form named, even if the answer is "none"?
- If the image has multiple parts, is there one continuous carrier line/ribbon/path?
- Do nodes hang from the carrier with clear primary/secondary hierarchy?
- Is there a text plan: L1/L2/L3 labels, placements, and draft-vs-overlay decision?
- Is the typography mode named: draft labels or publish overlay?
- Is the font feeling named?
- For Chinese text, was the mode chosen deliberately: model-rendered concise labels, or blank containers for overlay when dense/exact/editable text matters?
- Is the human presence level named: H0-H5?
- Is the charm level named: C0-C5?
- If using a human trace, role token, abstract figure, or storybook actor, does it clarify who acts, decides, waits, suffers, benefits, or learns?
- If using a Paper Operator, does it perform the core action, say what breaks if removed, and use a tool that fits the article domain?
- Are labels short and attached to objects?
- Are shots varied but stylistically compatible?
- Is the style clearly different from Xiaohei-like black mascot doodles?
- If using a visual identity system, is it non-character and based on stable marks?
- Are PrismGrid primitive geometry, symbol classes, semantic shape map, carrier glyph, label containers, micro chart form, and color roles defined?
- Is the package symbol legend used as an internal consistency rule rather than drawn as a large visible legend box?
- If the concept includes "user" or "human", is it represented without avatar/profile/head-and-shoulders pictograms?

## Generated Image QA

Accept if:

- The idea reads in three seconds.
- The composition preset is recognizable at first glance.
- It avoids generic AI output even before you read the prompt.
- The form is the simplest sufficient form for the idea.
- It passes the default-generation smoke when the skill itself is being validated: real article or concept, no private manual rescue, beauty score 4+.
- The image prevents a concrete reader misunderstanding.
- The visual does not overclaim the source article.
- There is one focal action.
- The visual structure is visible.
- The named diagram form is visible and appropriate.
- If spatial or 3D, depth clarifies hierarchy, distance, containment, or route; it is not decorative perspective.
- The form uses space well: clear gutters, quiet zone, focal module, and no equal-weight clutter.
- The visual harness is visible and coherent: the output family, diagram form, spatial mode, material, line behavior, color system, and composition engine agree with each other.
- The historical or design lineage is abstracted into composition, line, color, material, geometry, or rhythm rather than direct artist imitation.
- The style choice makes the article figure clearer or more memorable without weakening labels, tokens, or accuracy.
- Color roles are consistent: Passage for path, Evidence for proof, Risk for constraint, Growth for output, one Focus accent.
- Line hierarchy is visible: quiet grid, standard outlines, one stronger carrier, clear boundaries.
- Line patterns mean something: dashed future, dotted evidence, broken failure, wavy pressure, glow guide reveal.
- Labels live in containers and attach to objects, surfaces, zones, edges, or paths.
- Objects are connected semantically.
- The metaphor fits the article.
- Start, change, and result are visible.
- The main carrier or reader path is visible first.
- There is enough empty space.
- Quiet zones look deliberate, not empty by accident.
- Labels are short and attached.
- Line, chart, and label hierarchy support the same reader path.
- Same shapes carry the same meaning.
- It does not look like a PPT slide.
- It does not look like a Xiaohei copy.
- If using PrismGrid, the image is recognizable without any face, body, or recurring character.
- It does not look like a training slide, courseware handout, or generic process diagram.
- If the prompt called for a pyramid, matrix, map, timeline, loop, funnel, cutaway, ecosystem, scorecard, part-to-whole, storyboard, canvas, spatial stack, room, terrain, orbit, or object board, that form is instantly recognizable without feeling like clipart.
- If the prompt called for tree, Venn, Sankey, service blueprint, causal model, or value chain, the form obeys its accuracy rule: nesting, overlap, true quantity, lanes, causal evidence, or semantic axes.
- If it uses a guide object, the object has no face, eyes, limbs, or mascot behavior and helps reveal the structure.
- If it uses human presence, the human element is smaller than the diagram idea and supported by the source article.
- If it uses a Paper Operator, the operator physically operates a concrete object from the article domain: carrier, lens, gate, frame, palette, light card, archive folder, note, boundary screen, weather card, layer, map, or evidence object.
- Removing the operator would weaken the relation, reader path, or focal action.
- The operator's tool and metaphor world fit the article domain, rather than forcing engineering props into art, culture, life, or emotional essays.
- Beauty score is 4 or 5, not just "acceptable".
- There is one focal surprise that clarifies the idea.
- The material family is coherent: editorial paper model, field atlas plate, soft diagram infographic, spatial editorial model, or clear-line picturebook explainer.
- The image obeys one stability preset instead of blending several layouts.
- Anchor and label counts stay within the chosen preset.
- It does not mix glossy 3D, sketch lines, paper cutouts, UI cards, and photo realism in one image.
- It would still look intentional when placed inside an article column, not only as a standalone poster.

Regenerate if:

- It relies on generic AI/tech icons.
- It has no clear start/change/result.
- It does not match the figure spec.
- It chose a complex form when a single object, two-state contrast, or 3-step path would be clearer.
- It makes the article claim stronger, more certain, more causal, or more quantified than the source supports.
- It has disconnected panels or symbols with no carrier.
- It has equal-weight nodes and no hierarchy.
- It uses too many floating labels.
- It requires more than 8 labels to understand.
- It is merely decorative.
- It looks like generic AI output: stock icons, floating tech symbols, decorative gradients, fake tiny text, or a default isometric office scene.
- It passes the written spec but fails visually: no taste, weak margins, muddy texture, noisy background, or no focal hierarchy.
- It drifts away from the selected preset: adds a scene, extra panels, extra process steps, dashboard cards, extra roads, or unrelated layout machinery.
- It has more anchors or labels than the selected preset allows.
- It uses "in the style of X" as the whole visual direction.
- It imitates a living artist, contemporary copyrighted illustrator, named artwork, signature composition, or distinctive personal mark.
- It chooses a famous style because it is famous rather than because it clarifies the article.
- The style lineage overrides the selected diagram form.
- The output family is wrong for the placement, such as a cover-like poster when the article needs a mechanism, or a dense mechanism when the article needs a thesis anchor.
- It uses visual spectacle to conceal weak form selection.
- It has mascot characters doing nothing.
- It has an operator standing beside the diagram instead of operating it.
- It uses multiple operators when one would clarify the relation.
- It uses a black blob / white dot eyes / tiny worker as the central visual identity.
- It uses faces, limbs, or mascot behavior to create consistency.
- It uses head-and-shoulders user icons, profile heads, silhouettes, or generic stick figures.
- It adds a cute character when a hand, empty chair, role card, or object trace would be clearer.
- It repeats a figure across images as an accidental mascot.
- It uses cuteness that makes the article feel childish, unserious, or less accurate.
- Its symbols feel like unrelated stock icons rather than one system.
- It uses a fixed scene unrelated to the article.
- It uses charts or arrows as decoration rather than explanation.
- It chooses a generic flowchart when the article needed a pyramid, matrix, map, timeline, loop, funnel, cutaway, ecosystem, scorecard, part-to-whole, storyboard, framework canvas, or spatial diagram.
- It chooses Sankey/alluvial without real quantities.
- It uses a causal loop when the article only shows correlation or sequence.
- It uses Venn/Euler with meaningless overlap.
- It uses a tree/nesting form when categories are not actually hierarchical.
- It uses 3D perspective as spectacle without encoding hierarchy, distance, containment, or route.
- It uses hard square boxes and rigid triangles by habit when softer editorial shapes would fit better.
- It uses colors decoratively with no role.
- Every connector has the same line weight.
- The main carrier is weaker than secondary lines.
- Red marks both danger and ordinary emphasis in the same image.
- Dashed, dotted, broken, or wavy lines appear without semantic reason.
- Labels float in empty space without containers or attachment.
- It has text areas but no actual text hierarchy.
- It renders public-facing Chinese text badly when the plan required publish overlay mode.
- It forbids model-rendered Chinese by habit even when concise integrated labels would be clearer.
- It uses a font feeling that conflicts with the article mood or diagram role.
- It has a large visible legend box, bottom principle bar, or checkmarks under every step by default.
- It explains every detail and loses charm.
- It is accurate but lifeless and has no focal surprise.
- It is charming but the charm does not clarify the article.

## Default-Generation Smoke QA

Run this when the skill itself was edited or when the user complains that default output quality is weak.

- Use a real article or real concept, not a toy prompt.
- Let the skill choose the figure spec, diagram form, visual harness, image quality harness, and prompt.
- Let the skill choose exactly one composition stability preset and check whether the image obeys it.
- If the skill chooses an action character, check whether removing it would weaken the figure and whether the operator's tool fits the article domain.
- Do not manually rescue the prompt with private taste notes outside the skill.
- Generate one image.
- Score the image using `image-quality-harness.md` and `aesthetic-quality-bar.md`.
- If it looks like PPT, stock icons, generic AI art, a poster instead of an article figure, or a pretty but unclear metaphor, patch the skill before claiming improvement.
- Record the sample path and the largest remaining weakness.

## Delivery QA

For final delivery, include:

- image path
- intended article placement
- one-line purpose
- whether it is final, optional, or needs regeneration
- native text status: rendered in-image, intentionally no-label, or external overlay by explicit request
- if this is README/gallery/showcase output, whether at least one image proves the grammar works outside engineering/product topics
