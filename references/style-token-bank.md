# Style Token Bank

Use this bank after the structure and diagram form are clear. The default must not resemble Xiaohei-like white-background hand-drawn mascot illustrations.

Before choosing a style mode, choose a concrete diagram form from `diagram-form-catalog.md`. Style tokens should make the chosen form more legible and beautiful; they should not replace the form.

For broader style exploration, use `visual-harness-and-style-atlas.md` before this file. The harness chooses output family, spatial mode, style lineage, abstraction level, material, line behavior, color system, and composition engine. This bank then chooses the local surface tokens and metaphor world. Do not use a style lineage as a shortcut around the diagram form.

## New Default: Grid-Collage Editorial System

Default for most article body images:

- 16:9 horizontal.
- Editorial grid with visible alignment logic.
- Off-white, warm white, or very pale tinted background, not pure doodle-white by habit.
- Flat paper-cut or vector-collage objects with subtle texture.
- Geometric modules: cards, strips, blocks, frames, capsules, tiles, bands.
- Clear typography zones and blank label containers.
- 1 focal structure, 1 connection grammar, 3-6 text zones.
- 1 named diagram form from the catalog, with visible layout skeleton and spatial mode when useful.
- 2-3 color palette chosen for the article, not always red/orange/blue.
- No mascot as the central identity.
- No sketchy little black character.
- No "cute hand-drawn explainer" look.

## Style Modes

Style modes can pair with historical/design lineages, but the pairing must stay readable:

- Swiss / International Typographic Clarity pairs with PrismGrid, Explanation Engineering, Data-Object Hybrid, scorecards, and matrices.
- Bauhaus / Constructive Workshop pairs with Technical Plate, Instruction Manual, object boards, and workflow diagrams.
- De Stijl / Neo-Plastic Grid pairs with matrices, canvases, constraints, and structured arguments.
- Matisse Cutout Logic pairs with Grid-Collage Editorial, Soft Diagram Infographic, Paper Theater, and warm public explainers.
- Ukiyo-e-informed composition pairs with map, terrain, route, pressure field, and journey diagrams.
- Paul Klee symbolic micro-world pairs with Field Atlas, Zukan Card, and gentle taxonomy.
- Albers color interaction pairs with nested states, comparison, part-to-whole, and perception/tradeoff figures.
- Calder mobile balance pairs with dependency, ecosystem, orbit, and fragile-equilibrium figures.
- Art Nouveau organic line pairs with growth loops, emotional curves, and life-system journeys.
- Art Deco streamlined geometry pairs with poster-cards, premium summaries, pyramids, ladders, and city/market metaphors.
- Risograph editorial print pairs with newsletters, culture essays, approachable explainers, and field guides.

Never pair a lineage with a form if it breaks the article's accuracy rule. Cubist faceting is poor for exact quantitative charts; constructivist urgency is poor for calm evidence review; Art Deco confidence is poor for safety warnings.

### PrismGrid / Anchor Glyph System

Best for: any article package that needs a recognizable non-character visual identity.

Tokens: semantic anchor nodes, visible carrier glyph, label containers, stable shape meanings, five primitive geometries, restrained semantic palette, no face or body, one layout skeleton per image.

### Grid-Collage Editorial

Best for: broad essays, business analysis, product thinking, social commentary.

Tokens: modular grid, paper cutout shapes, large typographic label zones, strong negative space, color blocks, thin connector lines.

### Technical Plate

Best for: mechanisms, product teardown, workflows, security, architecture.

Tokens: clean ruled lines, numbered callouts, exploded parts, measurement marks, section cuts, blueprint-like discipline without looking like CAD.

### Data-Object Hybrid

Best for: arguments with evidence, trends, markets, behavioral patterns.

Tokens: charts become physical objects; bars as shelves, dots as beads, lines as paths, axes as scaffolds.

### Magazine Cutaway

Best for: complex systems, hidden layers, "inside X".

Tokens: cutaway window, stacked layers, cross-section, labeled compartments, bold editorial composition.

### Paper Theater

Best for: human situations, culture, personal essays.

Tokens: paper stage, flat props, silhouette-free figures only if needed, caption strips, visible layers of paper.

### Field Atlas

Best for: research, observations, trend reports, learning notes.

Tokens: specimen cards, map fragments, index numbers, legend box, pinned samples, controlled annotation.

### Zukan Card

Best for: concepts that need taxonomy, comparison, or "what this is made of" clarity.

Tokens: numbered specimen card, side notes, tiny scale mark, comparison row, calm editorial labels, no mascot.

### Instruction Manual

Best for: mechanisms, procedures, failures, and "how it works" explanations.

Tokens: part labels, stable camera, one action per step, wrong/right marks, thin ruled lines, minimal language.

### Explanation Engineering

Best for: analytical essays, AI/product/technical arguments, strategy notes.

Tokens: visible reasoning path, assumption card, local consequence, micro chart, object metaphor, disciplined callouts.

### Minimal Comic Grid

Best for: before/after, user pain, failure-to-insight.

Tokens: 2-4 equal panels, same framing, one variable changes, strong caption discipline. Avoid cute characters.

### Clear-Line Picture-Book Explainer

Best for: simple, interesting, child-readable article body illustrations that must not feel like AI clutter.

Tokens: clean consistent outlines, flat colors, no hatching, one focal relation, simple props, planned caption area, readable before text.

### Diagram Comic

Best for: product, technical, and business articles that need warmth without losing structure.

Tokens: visible diagram structure, picture-book objects, physical connectors, disciplined labels, one small visual twist.

### Soft Diagram Infographic

Best for: work/life systems, public-facing explainers, broad essays, and diagrams that need clarity without corporate stiffness.

Tokens: rounded irregular modules, calm grid, friendly labels, simple object metaphors, soft carriers, light texture, one warm accent plus one cool accent, generous whitespace.

Use with: pyramid, matrix, map, timeline, loop, funnel, part-to-whole, canvas, spatial stack, room diagram, terrain model, object board.

Avoid: decorative cute icons, emoji-like symbols, generic slide templates, equal square-card grids.

## Character Rule

Characters are optional and controlled by `human-presence-and-charm-system.md`. If used:

- keep them abstract, flat, and secondary
- no black blob body
- no tiny mascot with dot eyes
- no "standing beside diagram" pose
- no recurring character as the skill identity

If the article needs a character to perform a diagram action, use `action-character-system.md` and choose one Paper Operator:

- universal/system: Thread Runner, Lens Keeper, Gate Builder.
- art/design: Frame Setter, Color Tuner, Light Catcher.
- culture/history: Archive Tender.
- personal/life: Care Folder, Boundary Keeper, Weather Reader.
- business/product/technical: Stack Mason, Residue Sweeper, Tradeoff Weigher.

These operators are not mascots. They are faceless paper-and-tool figures that make the relation visible. Their tools must match the article domain; do not use engineering gates and nodes by default for art, culture, life, or emotional essays.

Prefer object-first storytelling: cards, blocks, routes, containers, layers, maps, charts, and cutaways.

Human presence scale:

- H0: no human
- H1: human trace, such as hand, empty chair, footprint, cursor, used object
- H2: role token, such as role tab, badge, audience slice, decision desk
- H3: abstract faceless figure only when posture/action clarifies meaning
- H4: storybook/paper-theater actor only when the article is about a human situation
- H5: mascot/recurring character only by explicit request

Charm scale:

- C0: austere
- C1: warm
- C2: playful
- C3: storybook
- C4: characterful
- C5: mascot-led, explicit request only

Default: H0-H2 and C1-C2. For technical or analytical pieces, prefer H0-H1. For personal essays or culture commentary, H2-H4 may be appropriate.

Do not use generic stick figures as the default. They often read as cheap whiteboard training diagrams. If a simple person is necessary, use a faceless paper-cut figure, a hand-only actor, or a role token.

If the user asks for a comic or more charm, prefer `Clear-Line Picture-Book Explainer`, `2x2 Yonkoma Explainer`, or `Diagram Comic` from `comic-picturebook-forms.md` instead of anime/manga surface style.

## Optional Guide Object

When the article package needs a stronger identity, use a guide object instead of a mascot:

- Prism Lantern: a small faceted lamp that reveals the structure with light paths
- Folded Compass: an origami-like pointer that marks reader direction
- Index Lens: a transparent rounded lens that frames the focal relation
- Signal Thread: a recurring colored cord that becomes the carrier
- Anchor Tile: a small recurring package mark attached to labels

Rules:

- no face, eyes, limbs, or character behavior
- secondary to the diagram form
- used to reveal, point, frame, connect, or mark
- optional; do not force it into every image
- never "standing beside" the diagram

## Typography Feeling

Default typography should feel editorial and designed:

- clean sans-serif Chinese feeling
- structured label boxes
- high contrast between focal label and small labels
- no casual handwritten doodle labels by default
- publish mode should overlay real text after image generation

## Anti-Cliche / Anti-Copy List

Avoid by default:

- Xiaohei-like black small figures
- hand-drawn whiteboard doodle style as the default
- black line-art mascots
- generic AI robot, brain, rocket, lightbulb, cloud
- business people shaking hands
- generic funnel diagram
- floating dashboards
- dense symbolic collage with no reading path
- direct artist-copy prompts
- living artist or contemporary illustrator imitation
- famous artwork layouts as article diagrams
- historical style used as decoration without relation to the article job

## Difference Matrix From Xiaohei-Like Work

Use this when an image risks drifting into imitation:

| Dimension | Avoid | Prefer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identity | recurring black blob mascot | object system, cards, routes, charts, cutaways |
| Eyes / body | white dot eyes, tiny legs | no character, or secondary abstract figures |
| Line | casual wobbly whiteboard doodle as identity | clean-line, ruled, editorial, or picture-book line based on article |
| Background | pure white as the whole brand | off-white, pale tint, field-card, manual page, or clean editorial canvas |
| Color | fixed red/orange/blue handwriting | 1-3 semantic colors chosen per article |
| Text | handwritten notes everywhere | structured labels, caption strips, tags, badges, blank overlay zones |
| Composition | small character performs one bizarre action | reader path with L0-L3 hierarchy and optional chart forms |
| Charm | mascot absurdity | clarity, physical metaphor, small visual twist |

| Guide identity | cute mascot guide | Prism Lantern, Folded Compass, Index Lens, Signal Thread, Anchor Tile |

## PrismGrid Quick Spec

Use this when the user asks for an original non-character system:

- Identity comes from semantic marks, not characters.
- Primitive geometry stays limited: rounded rectangle, circle/dot, arc, wedge, line/polyline.
- Anchor nodes carry main ideas.
- Carrier glyphs create reader path.
- Label containers hold all text.
- State stamps mark pass/fail/risk/done.
- Evidence slips or dots make proof visible.
- Boundary frames show permission, review, or trust.
- Containers show memory, grouping, context, or ownership.
- Tension marks show pressure, tradeoff, or mismatch.
- Transformation marks show input-action-output.
- Optional guide objects reveal the form but do not act like characters.

Default prompt phrase:

```text
PrismGrid Symbol System, original non-character editorial symbol system, semantic anchor nodes, visible carrier glyph, structured label containers, five primitive geometries, stable shape meanings, restrained semantic colors, no mascot, no faces, no limbs
```
